The Golden Law: The Accounting Equation

The Absolute Truth: What are Debits & Credits?

Many people struggle with accounting because of bank debit cards. When the bank says "we are crediting your account," it sounds like positive news, and a debit feels bad. **Discard those assumptions immediately!**

DR
Debit

Just Means: "LEFT SIDE"

Derived from the Latin word **debere** (meaning "to owe"), which was abbreviated as **Dr.** in early Italian ledgers. It simply marks the **left hand column** of any account paper.

Located on the Left Column
CR
Credit

Just Means: "RIGHT SIDE"

Derived from the Latin word **credere** (meaning "to trust"), abbreviated as **Cr.** It marks the **right hand column** of account ledgers. It represents how value is source-credited.

Located on the Right Column

Professor Higgins' Golden Rule:

A debit does not mean an increase, nor does a credit mean a decrease. Whether an account increases or decreases with a debit/credit depends entirely on what **type of account** it is! Let's check the next tab to see the exact pattern.

The Cheat Code: The D.E.A. / L.E.R. Framework

How do we remember which account goes up with a Debit and which goes up with a Credit? We use the easy, universally trusted acronym: **DEALER**.

Group 1: D - E - A

Normal Debit

These accounts increase on the LEFT (Debit)

D

Drawings / Dividends

Value taken out by the owners.

E

Expenses

Costs of running business (Rent, utilities).

A

Assets

Valuable items owned (Cash, equipment).

Group 2: L - E - R

Normal Credit

These accounts increase on the RIGHT (Credit)

L

Liabilities

Money the business owes to outsiders.

E

Equity

The owner's stake in the business.

R

Revenue

Earnings from sales or services.
